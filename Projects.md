## Columbia PhD — RNA splicing methods

**[LeafletFA](https://github.com/daklab/Leaflet-analysis)** — Atlas-scale Bayesian factor model for alternative splicing in single cells. Applied to multisample Smart-seq2 datasets to identify conserved age-associated splicing programs across mouse and human tissues. Manuscript under review at *Nature Aging* (2025).

**[SpliceVI](https://github.com/daklab/SpliceVI)** — Variational autoencoder for joint modeling of single-cell splicing and gene expression, enabling robust integration of sparse splicing data. Pre-print: [bioRxiv, 2025](https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2025.11.26.690853v1.abstract).

**[LeafletSC](https://github.com/daklab/LeafletSC)** — Python package for alternative splicing analysis in single-cell RNA-seq using binomial mixture models.

**[Isoviz](https://github.com/daklab/isoviz)** — R package for visualizing splice junctions across transcript isoforms, with built-in support for designing gRNA sequences targeting junctions of interest using CRISPR/Cas13.

**[Orthrus](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11482885/)** — Contributed to an evolutionary and functional RNA foundation model. Accepted at *Nature Methods*, 2026.

## Lymphoma research — UHN Princess Margaret

Worked in [Dr. Robert Kridel's lab](http://kridel-lab.ca/) at [UHN](https://www.uhnresearch.ca/institutes/pm), Toronto (May 2019 – July 2021).

**[NHL-ASCT-PI](https://lymphoma.shinyapps.io/NHL-ASCT-PI/)** — Shiny web app for patient risk modeling in non-Hodgkin lymphoma, integrating clinical factors and pretransplant PET imaging. Published in [*Blood Advances*, 2020](https://ashpublications.org/bloodadvances/article/4/22/5762/474205/).

Contributed to multi-omic integration studies in diffuse large B-cell lymphoma combining RNA-seq, ChIP-seq, and CRISPR/Cas9 screening ([*Clinical Cancer Research*, 2021](https://aacrjournals.org/clincancerres/article-abstract/27/19/5401/671692/Combined-EZH2-Inhibition-and-IKAROS-Degradation)) and longitudinal tumor evolution analyses ([*Haematologica*, 2021](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8094129/); [2022](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9827161/)).
